What is a Destiny Sherpa?

The word 'sherpa' originates from people of Tibetan descent guiding and helping people on mountain-climbing expeditions. How does this translate into Destiny?

Destiny Sherpas are similar in nature that they help people accomplish the most difficult content you can do in the game, by teaching and guiding them how to do it without any pressure. Most commonly Sherpas teach the multitude of Raids the game has to offer, but some do also teach other harder activities in the game.

Why You Need a Sherpa to Help Shape Your Destiny

Raids are the pinnacle of end-game content in Destiny. The most prestigeous, some of the most fun and the most rewarding content with some of the best weapons and armor you can only get ahold of in the raids!

They are epic activities with fun, sometimes crazy jumping puzzles and incredible boss-fights/encounters with punishing mechanics that will test your skills and it's an experience you won't find anywhere else in the game, or most other games infact. However, there is a small problem for some people that stops them from taking part in these awesome experiences.

The main problem is that raids require you to have 5 other people to play with, and there is no match-making feature that will find people automatically for you. Having 5 friends playing the same game is not always so easy or realistic, let alone getting them all to be online at the same time. Of course there is tools to solve this problem like using LFG channels (Looking For Group) to find people to play with, but they don't exactly offer a good experience for people who don't have any experience doing raids before.
Most people on LFG want and prefer to have experienced players along with them for a faster or smoother experience as raids are not simple in nature, they require you to know how to solve the mechanics as well as being able to communicate and work with your teammates and the failure of 1 member is usually reflected on the entire team. This leaves people to wanting or even expecting to play with others who know whats going on, and being unwelcoming to people who don't.

Finding a clan to play with is also another option which usually have a more friendly environement compared to playing with random people you find on LFG, but finding a clan that suits your needs can be a whole other task in and of itself

So it can be quite the hostile environment for those who haven't done the raids prior but want to learn, and this is where we come in.
